Welcome to EE476: Introduction to VLSI Systems

Course and Lab Instructor: Rehan Naeem

Very Large Scale Integration (VLSI) is the process of creating integrated circuits by combining millions (or even billions) of transistors onto a single chip. VLSI technology has enabled the design of powerful microprocessors, memory devices, and system-on-chip solutions that drive modern electronics. It plays a critical role in advancing computing performance, reducing power consumption, and enabling compact, cost-effective electronic systems.

This course introduces the principles and practices of VLSI system design, from technology scaling to architecture optimization. Students explore device‑, circuit‑, and system‑level trade‑offs through targeted design and analysis tasks. Emphasis is placed on performance, power, and area (PPA) optimization using HDL‑based implementations. By the end, students can design, analyze, and propose effective solutions for advanced VLSI systems.
